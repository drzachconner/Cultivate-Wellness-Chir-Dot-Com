How Subluxations Affect Sleep

Subluxations are misalignments in the spine that create interference in the nervous system. When it comes to sleep, subluxations can affect rest in several ways:

Upper Cervical Involvement

The top bones of the neck (C1 and C2) sit directly beneath the brainstem, which houses the sleep-wake centers. Misalignment here can:

  • Disrupt signals that regulate sleep cycles
  • Affect blood flow to the brain
  • Create chronic tension that prevents relaxation
  • Interfere with cerebrospinal fluid circulation

Sympathetic Overdrive

Spinal stress often puts the body in a constant state of "fight or flight":

  • Elevated cortisol levels interfere with melatonin
  • Muscles remain tense even when trying to rest
  • The mind races and can't "shut off"
  • The body stays on alert instead of relaxing

Physical Discomfort

Structural issues create sleep-disrupting discomfort:

  • Neck and back pain that worsens when lying down
  • Difficulty finding a comfortable position
  • Numbness or tingling in arms or legs
  • Headaches that interfere with sleep

The Parasympathetic Nervous System Role

Your "Rest and Digest" System

The parasympathetic nervous system is your body's relaxation mode. For quality sleep, this system must be able to fully activate. Here's what happens when it works properly:

  • Heart rate slows: Calm, steady rhythm prepares body for rest
  • Breathing deepens: Slow, relaxed breathing signals safety to the brain
  • Muscles relax: Physical tension releases throughout the body
  • Digestion activates: Energy redirects from "survival" to "restoration"
  • Hormones shift: Melatonin production increases, cortisol decreases

Talsky Tonal Chiropractic (TTC) adjustments help remove nervous system interference, allowing the parasympathetic system to function optimally and supporting the body's natural ability to transition into restful sleep.

Children's Sleep Issues

Why Children Often Struggle with Sleep

Sleep problems in children are extremely common and can significantly impact development, behavior, and family wellbeing. Children may experience sleep issues due to:

  • Birth trauma: The birth process can create spinal stress affecting the nervous system
  • Developmental changes: Growing bodies experience frequent structural shifts
  • Sensory overload: An overwhelmed nervous system can't settle for sleep
  • Screen exposure: Technology affects the developing nervous system
  • Stress and anxiety: Even young children experience nervous system stress

Common childhood sleep issues we see include:

  • Difficulty settling at bedtime
  • Frequent night waking
  • Night terrors and nightmares
  • Bedwetting beyond typical age
  • Restless sleep with constant movement
  • Morning crankiness despite adequate sleep time

Gentle chiropractic care for children can help their nervous systems find balance, supporting more peaceful sleep naturally.
