The Nervous System and Immune Function Connection

A Two-Way Communication Highway

Your brain and immune system aren't separate entities—they're deeply interconnected partners in keeping you healthy. Here's how they work together:

  • The vagus nerve connects your brain directly to major immune organs including the spleen, thymus, and lymph nodes
  • Neurotransmitters released by nerves can either stimulate or suppress immune cell activity
  • The sympathetic nervous system (fight-or-flight) can suppress immune function when chronically activated
  • The parasympathetic nervous system (rest-and-digest) supports immune function and healing

This field, known as psychoneuroimmunology, demonstrates that nervous system health directly impacts immune competence.

How Subluxations Affect Immunity

A subluxation occurs when spinal bones become misaligned and interfere with proper nerve function. Here's how this can impact your immune system:

Disrupted Communication

When vertebrae are misaligned, they can create pressure or irritation on the nerves that exit the spine. These nerves carry vital signals between the brain and immune organs. Interference at this level can mean delayed or inappropriate immune responses.

Stress Response Activation

Subluxations can trigger a chronic low-grade stress response in the body. When the nervous system is stuck in "fight-or-flight" mode, resources are diverted away from immune function. The body prioritizes immediate survival over long-term health maintenance.

Inflammatory Impact

Nerve interference can contribute to systemic inflammation—a state where the immune system becomes overactive in some ways and underactive in others. This imbalance can make you more susceptible to both infections and inflammatory conditions.

Lymphatic Function

The lymphatic system, which carries immune cells throughout your body, relies on proper muscle movement and nerve control to function. Spinal misalignments can impair lymphatic drainage, reducing your body's ability to mount effective immune responses.

Research on Chiropractic and Immune Function

Scientific Studies Support the Connection

Research has explored the relationship between chiropractic care and immune function:

  • Immune cell enhancement: Studies have shown that chiropractic adjustments can increase levels of certain immune cells and antibodies

  • Respiratory illness: Research indicates regular chiropractic patients report fewer colds and faster recovery times
  • Inflammatory markers: Studies show adjustments can reduce pro-inflammatory cytokines while supporting anti-inflammatory responses
  • Autonomic balance: Research demonstrates that adjustments help shift the nervous system from stress-dominant to healing-dominant states

While more research is always valuable, the existing evidence points to a meaningful connection between spinal health and immune competence.
