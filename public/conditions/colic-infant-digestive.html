  • Crying for more than 3 hours per day
  • Occurring at least 3 days per week

The Impact on Families

You're Not Alone

Colic doesn't just affect babies. It takes a tremendous toll on the entire family:

  • Parental exhaustion: Sleep deprivation from constant nighttime waking
  • Emotional stress: Feelings of helplessness, anxiety, and frustration
  • Relationship strain: Tension between partners from the stress
  • Breastfeeding challenges: Difficulty establishing nursing due to fussiness
  • Sibling impact: Less attention available for other children
  • Postpartum mental health: Increased risk of depression and anxiety

Finding relief for your baby means finding relief for your whole family.

The Nervous System Connection to Digestion

Why Nervous System Function Matters

Your baby's digestive system is controlled by the nervous system. When there's interference in the nerve pathways, digestion can become inefficient and uncomfortable:

  • Gut motility: The nervous system controls how food moves through the digestive tract
  • Enzyme production: Proper nerve signals trigger digestive enzyme release
  • Muscle coordination: The stomach and intestines need precise nerve control to function
  • Stress response: A stressed nervous system affects digestion directly

Birth can be stressful on a baby's delicate spine and nervous system, potentially creating interference that affects how well their body functions.

The Vagus Nerve: The Digestion Superhighway

Your Baby's "Rest and Digest" Control Center

The vagus nerve is the longest cranial nerve in the body, running from the brain stem through the neck and into the abdomen. It plays a critical role in:

  • Stomach function: Controls acid production and stomach emptying
  • Intestinal movement: Regulates peristalsis (the wave-like muscle contractions)
  • Inflammation control: Helps regulate the gut's inflammatory response
  • Relaxation response: Activates the "rest and digest" parasympathetic state

The vagus nerve passes through the upper cervical spine. Misalignments in this area can affect vagus nerve function, potentially contributing to digestive symptoms in infants.

Gentle Infant Adjustments

Parents often ask, "Is chiropractic safe for babies?" The answer is absolutely yes. Infant adjustments are nothing like adult adjustments.

What Infant Chiropractic Looks Like

Using Talsky Tonal Chiropractic (TTC) techniques, our approach is incredibly gentle:

  • No twisting or cracking: Gentle sustained contact, not manipulation
  • Baby stays comfortable: Many infants sleep through their adjustments
  • Brief sessions: Appointments are quick to minimize disruption

Our Assessment Process

Before any care begins, we perform a thorough evaluation:

  • Complete birth and health history
  • Gentle palpation to identify areas of tension
  • Assessment of spinal movement and posture
  • Evaluation of nervous system function
  • Discussion of feeding, sleeping, and symptom patterns

Areas We Focus On

For digestive issues, we pay particular attention to:

  • Upper cervical spine: Where the vagus nerve passes through
  • Mid-back (thoracic): Nerve supply to the stomach and upper digestive tract
  • Lower back (lumbar/sacral): Nerve supply to the intestines
  • Overall spinal tension patterns: From the birthing process

Common Questions from Parents

How soon can my newborn be checked?

Babies can be evaluated from birth. In fact, the sooner we can identify and address any nervous system interference, the better. Many parents bring their newborns in within the first few weeks of life.

How many visits will my baby need?

This varies based on your baby's specific situation. Some infants respond quickly with just a few visits, while others benefit from ongoing care. We'll create an individualized care plan based on our findings.

Will it hurt my baby?

No. The gentle pressure used is comfortable for babies. Most infants relax during their adjustments, and many even fall asleep.

Is this safe?

Yes. Infant chiropractic care has an excellent safety record. The techniques used are specifically designed for babies' delicate structures.

When to Seek Care

Consider bringing your baby in if you notice:

  • Inconsolable crying episodes, especially in the evening
  • Difficulty with breastfeeding or bottle feeding
  • Excessive spitting up or reflux symptoms
  • Gas, bloating, or constipation
  • Difficulty sleeping or staying asleep
  • Preference for turning head to one side
  • Arching of the back during or after feeding
  • General fussiness or discomfort
