Understanding the Nervous System Connection

Why Nervous System Care Matters

Children with autism and neurodevelopmental differences often have nervous systems that are:

  • Hypersensitive to input: Lights, sounds, textures, and social situations can feel overwhelming
  • Stuck in stress response: The "fight or flight" system may be chronically activated
  • Struggling with regulation: Difficulty calming down, transitioning, or adapting to changes
  • Processing information differently: Unique patterns of sensory integration and motor planning

Chiropractic care doesn't treat autism‚Äîit supports the nervous system to function more optimally, which can help children better handle the world around them.

Conditions We Support

Our Neurodevelopmental Focus

We work with children experiencing:

  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) - From mild to more significant presentations
  • Sensory Processing Disorder (SPD) - Over or under-responsive to sensory input
  • ADHD - Attention, hyperactivity, and impulse challenges
  • Developmental delays - Motor, speech, or cognitive milestones
  • Anxiety and emotional dysregulation - Especially sensory-related anxiety
  • Sleep difficulties - Common in neurodivergent children
  • Digestive issues - The gut-brain connection is often involved
  • Apraxia and motor planning challenges - Coordination and movement difficulties

What Parents Often Notice

While every child responds differently, parents commonly report improvements in:

  • Sensory processing: Less overwhelm, better tolerance of various inputs
  • Sleep quality: Falling asleep easier, staying asleep longer
  • Digestive function: Better regularity, less discomfort, expanded food acceptance
  • Emotional regulation: Fewer meltdowns, faster recovery when upset
  • Transitions: Smoother changes between activities and environments
  • Communication: More eye contact, increased verbalizations
  • Body awareness: Better coordination and motor planning
  • Overall calmness: More regulated baseline nervous system state

Complementary Care Approach

We believe in team-based support. Chiropractic care works beautifully alongside:

  • Occupational Therapy (OT) - Sensory integration, fine motor skills
  • Speech-Language Therapy - Communication and feeding support
  • Physical Therapy (PT) - Gross motor development
  • ABA or developmental therapies - Behavioral and skill building
  • Nutritional interventions - Addressing gut-brain connections
  • Mental health support - For child and family

We're happy to communicate with your child's therapy team to coordinate care and share progress.

Research & Evidence

Research into chiropractic care for neurodevelopmental conditions continues to grow.
