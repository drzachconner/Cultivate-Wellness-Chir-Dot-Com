Types of Headaches We Address

Tension Headaches

The most common type, often described as a tight band around the head. Usually caused by muscle tension in the neck and shoulders, often related to poor posture, stress, or spinal misalignment.

Symptoms: Dull, aching pain; pressure on both sides of head; neck and shoulder tightness

Migraines

Neurological events that can cause intense, throbbing pain, often on one side of the head. May be accompanied by sensitivity to light, sound, nausea, and visual disturbances (aura).

Symptoms: Pulsating pain; light/sound sensitivity; nausea; visual changes; lasting 4-72 hours

Cervicogenic Headaches

Headaches that originate from problems in the cervical spine (neck). Pain typically starts in the neck and radiates to the head. These respond particularly well to chiropractic care.

Symptoms: Pain starting in neck; one-sided head pain; reduced neck mobility; triggered by neck movement

The Cervical Spine Connection

Why Your Neck Matters

The cervical spine (neck) plays a crucial role in headache development. Here's why:

  • Nerve supply: The upper cervical nerves (C1-C3) directly influence the head, face, and scalp
  • Blood flow: Vertebral arteries pass through the cervical vertebrae, supplying blood to the brain
  • Muscle connections: Suboccipital muscles at the skull base can refer pain into the head
  • Dural tension: The spinal cord covering (dura mater) attaches to the upper neck‚Äîtension here can trigger headaches
  • Brainstem proximity: The brain stem, which regulates pain perception, sits just above C1

When the cervical spine is misaligned or restricted, it can irritate these structures and contribute to headache patterns.

When to Seek Immediate Medical Attention

While most headaches respond well to conservative care, certain headaches require immediate medical evaluation:

  • Sudden, severe headache (worst of your life)
  • Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion, or seizures
  • Headache after head injury
  • Headache with vision changes, slurred speech, or weakness
  • New headache pattern after age 50

If you experience any of these symptoms, please seek emergency care.

Prevention Strategies

Daily Habits That Help

In addition to chiropractic care, these lifestyle factors support headache prevention:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink adequate water throughout the day
  • Maintain regular sleep: Consistent bedtime and wake time
  • Take screen breaks: Every 20-30 minutes, look away and move
  • Practice good posture: Especially when working at a computer
  • Manage stress: Regular relaxation, breathing exercises, or meditation
  • Move regularly: Light exercise helps reduce muscle tension
  • Track your triggers: Keep a headache diary to identify patterns
  • Maintain regular meals: Blood sugar drops can trigger headaches
