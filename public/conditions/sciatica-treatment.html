What Is Sciatica?

Understanding the Sciatic Nerve

The sciatic nerve is the longest and largest nerve in your body, running from your lower back through your hips and buttocks and down each leg. When this nerve becomes compressed, irritated, or inflamed, it can cause pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness along its path.

Sciatica is not a diagnosis itself but rather a symptom of an underlying condition affecting the sciatic nerve. The pain typically affects only one side of the body and can range from a dull ache to sharp, shooting sensations that make sitting, standing, or walking difficult.

Common Causes of Sciatica

Disc Problems (Herniated or Bulging Discs)

The most common cause of sciatica occurs when the soft, gel-like center of a spinal disc pushes through a crack in the tough outer layer, putting pressure on the sciatic nerve roots. This can happen from:

  • Age-related wear and tear on spinal discs
  • Lifting heavy objects with improper form
  • Sudden movements or trauma
  • Prolonged sitting with poor posture

Piriformis Syndrome

The piriformis muscle, located deep in the buttock, can tighten or spasm and compress the sciatic nerve as it passes beneath or through the muscle. This often results from:

  • Prolonged sitting (especially on hard surfaces)
  • Overuse from running or other repetitive activities
  • Hip injuries or trauma
  • Muscle imbalances in the pelvis and hips

Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal can put pressure on the nerves, including the sciatic nerve. This condition is often associated with:

  • Age-related changes in the spine
  • Bone spurs from osteoarthritis
  • Thickened ligaments in the spinal canal
  • Degenerative disc disease

Prevention Tips

While we can help address existing sciatica, these strategies may help prevent recurrence:

  • Maintain good posture: Especially when sitting for long periods
  • Stay active: Regular movement keeps the spine healthy
  • Lift properly: Bend at the knees, keep the back straight
  • Take breaks: Stand and stretch every 30-60 minutes when sitting
  • Strengthen your core: A strong core supports the lower back
  • Maintain healthy weight: Excess weight increases spinal stress
  • Sleep smart: Use a supportive mattress and proper sleep position
  • Regular chiropractic care: Maintain proper spinal alignment before problems develop
