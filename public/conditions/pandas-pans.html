What Are PANDAS and PANS?

Understanding These Conditions

PANDAS (Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsychiatric Disorders Associated with Streptococcal Infections) and PANS (Pediatric Acute-onset Neuropsychiatric Syndrome) are conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks the brain following an infection.

  • PANDAS: Specifically triggered by strep infections (strep throat, scarlet fever)
  • PANS: Can be triggered by various infections (flu, Lyme, mycoplasma, sinus infections) or other immune triggers
  • Mechanism: Antibodies created to fight infection cross-react with brain tissue, particularly the basal ganglia
  • Result: Inflammation in the brain causing sudden psychiatric and neurological symptoms

When to Seek Medical Evaluation

If your child experiences sudden onset of any of these symptoms, especially following an illness, seek evaluation from a physician familiar with PANDAS/PANS. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial for better outcomes.

The Nervous System Connection

PANDAS and PANS fundamentally affect the nervous system through brain inflammation. This creates a cascade of dysfunction:

How the Nervous System is Affected

  • Basal ganglia inflammation: This brain region controls movement, behavior, and emotional regulation
  • Autonomic dysregulation: The body's "fight or flight" system becomes overactive
  • Vagus nerve dysfunction: Impaired communication between brain, gut, and immune system
  • Heightened stress response: The nervous system becomes stuck in a state of hypervigilance
  • Impaired neurological tone: Difficulty regulating emotional and physical responses

Working With Your Medical Team

Chiropractic care is a complementary approach that works alongside medical treatment, not as a replacement. Medical interventions for PANDAS/PANS may include:

  • Antibiotics to address underlying infection
  • Anti-inflammatory treatments
  • IVIG (intravenous immunoglobulin) therapy
  • Plasmapheresis in severe cases
  • Psychiatric medications to manage symptoms
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy

We're happy to coordinate with your child's physicians, immunologists, and mental health providers to provide integrated support.
