Understanding ADHD & the Nervous System

The Brain-Body Connection

ADHD is fundamentally a neurological condition affecting how the brain processes information, regulates attention, and controls impulses. Research shows that individuals with ADHD often have:

  • Dysregulated autonomic nervous system: Difficulty shifting between "fight or flight" and "rest and digest"
  • Altered brain wave patterns: Different theta/beta ratios affecting focus
  • Impaired prefrontal cortex function: Affecting executive function and impulse control
  • Sensory processing differences: Difficulty filtering relevant from irrelevant stimuli

Chiropractic care supports better nervous system function by removing interference and improving brain-body communication.

How Chiropractic Helps with Focus

The Upper Cervical Connection

The upper neck (C1-C2 vertebrae) is particularly critical for brain function:

  • Misalignments here can affect cerebrospinal fluid flow
  • Nerve irritation in this area impacts whole-body regulation
