How Anxiety Affects the Nervous System

The Stress Response Connection

Your nervous system is designed to protect you from danger through the stress response. However, in today's world, this ancient survival mechanism often becomes overactive:

  • Chronic activation: The nervous system perceives everyday stressors as threats
  • Chemical cascade: Constant release of stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline
  • Physical tension: Muscles stay contracted, especially in the neck and shoulders
  • Altered breathing: Shallow, rapid breathing that reinforces the stress cycle

When this becomes your body's default state, anxiety symptoms become chronic rather than occasional.

Fight or Flight vs. Rest and Digest

Your autonomic nervous system has two main branches that should work in balance:

Sympathetic: Fight or Flight

  • Heart rate increases
  • Breathing becomes shallow
  • Muscles tense for action
  • Digestion slows or stops
  • Sleep becomes difficult
  • Mind races with worry
  • Energy directed to survival

Parasympathetic: Rest and Digest

  • Heart rate slows
  • Breathing deepens
  • Muscles relax
  • Digestion functions properly
  • Sleep comes naturally
  • Mind feels clear and calm
  • Energy goes to healing

For many people dealing with anxiety, the sympathetic system dominates even when there's no real danger. The nervous system gets "stuck" in fight or flight mode, unable to shift into the calm, restorative state your body needs.

The Vagus Nerve Connection

Your Body's "Calm Down" Pathway

The vagus nerve is the longest cranial nerve in your body, running from your brain stem through your neck and into your chest and abdomen. It's the primary pathway for activating your parasympathetic (rest and digest) response.

Why this matters: The vagus nerve passes directly through the upper cervical spine (the top of your neck). Misalignments or tension in this area can interfere with vagus nerve function, making it harder for your body to shift out of stress mode.

By addressing spinal alignment and reducing nervous system interference, chiropractic care supports healthy vagus nerve function and your body's natural ability to calm itself.
