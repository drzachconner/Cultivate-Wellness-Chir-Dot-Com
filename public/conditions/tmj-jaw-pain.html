Understanding TMJ Dysfunction

The temporomandibular joint is one of the most complex joints in your body, connecting your lower jaw (mandible) to your skull. It allows you to open and close your mouth, chew, speak, and yawn. When this joint becomes dysfunctional, it can create a cascade of symptoms throughout your head, neck, and even shoulders.

TMJ dysfunction (also called TMD or TMJD) occurs when there's an imbalance in the muscles, ligaments, or structures supporting the jaw. This can result from:

  • Trauma to the jaw or head
  • Chronic stress and teeth grinding (bruxism)
  • Poor posture, especially forward head posture
  • Cervical spine misalignment
  • Muscle tension in the neck and shoulders
  • Dental issues or bite misalignment
  • Arthritis affecting the joint

The Cervical Spine and Jaw Connection

Why Your Neck Affects Your Jaw

The relationship between your cervical spine (neck) and your jaw is profound and often underappreciated:

  • Shared nerve supply: The trigeminal nerve (responsible for jaw sensation and movement) connects closely with upper cervical nerves
  • Muscle connections: Many muscles attach to both the jaw and cervical spine, creating functional linkage
  • Postural influence: Forward head posture changes jaw mechanics and increases TMJ stress
  • Nerve interference: Subluxations in the upper cervical spine can affect signals to and from the jaw

Research shows that cervical spine dysfunction is present in a significant percentage of TMJ patients. Addressing both areas often yields better results than treating the jaw alone.

Our Approach to TMJ Relief

2. Talsky Tonal Chiropractic (TTC) Care

TTC uses light, precise contacts rather than forceful manipulation, making it particularly appropriate for the sensitive cervical region.

Complementary Care with Dentists

Working Together for Your Best Outcome

TMJ dysfunction often benefits from a collaborative approach. Chiropractic care works well alongside:

  • Dentists and orthodontists: Addressing bite issues, providing night guards
  • Oral surgeons: For cases requiring surgical consultation
  • Physical therapists: For jaw-specific exercises and manual therapy
  • Massage therapists: For muscle tension release
  • Stress management specialists: For chronic tension and bruxism

We're happy to coordinate with your dental provider to ensure comprehensive care. Many dentists refer patients to us when they recognize that cervical spine issues are contributing to TMJ symptoms.
