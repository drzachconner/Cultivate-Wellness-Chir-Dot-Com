How Lyme Affects the Nervous System

The Neurological Impact of Lyme

Lyme disease, caused by Borrelia burgdorferi bacteria, has a particular affinity for nervous system tissue. This can lead to widespread neurological involvement:

  • Central nervous system inflammation: Affecting brain and spinal cord function
  • Peripheral nerve damage: Causing numbness, tingling, and pain in extremities
  • Autonomic dysfunction: Disrupting heart rate, digestion, and temperature regulation
  • Cranial nerve involvement: Leading to facial palsy, vision changes, or hearing issues
  • Brain fog and cognitive changes: Affecting memory, concentration, and mental clarity

Supporting optimal nervous system function is crucial for Lyme patients navigating these complex symptoms.

Supporting Immune Function

The Nervous System-Immune Connection

Your nervous system and immune system are in constant communication. When your spine is properly aligned and your nervous system functions optimally:

  • Immune cells receive clearer signals from the brain

Every Lyme patient's journey is different. We adapt our care based on:
