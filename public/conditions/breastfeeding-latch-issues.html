Cranial Nerve Involvement

The Neurology of Nursing

Successful breastfeeding requires the coordinated function of several cranial nerves that control the complex movements of sucking, swallowing, and breathing:

  • Trigeminal Nerve (CN V): Controls jaw movement and sensation in the mouth
  • Facial Nerve (CN VII): Controls lip and cheek muscles for creating seal
  • Glossopharyngeal Nerve (CN IX): Controls tongue movement and swallowing
  • Vagus Nerve (CN X): Coordinates swallowing and breathing
  • Hypoglossal Nerve (CN XII): Controls tongue movement essential for milk extraction

When spinal misalignments affect the upper cervical area where these nerves originate, nursing difficulties often result.

Birth Trauma Effects

The birth process, while natural, can place significant stress on a baby's delicate spine and nervous system. This is especially true in cases of:

  • Prolonged labor: Extended time in the birth canal can cause cervical strain
  • Fast delivery: Rapid descent may not allow tissues to adapt gradually
  • Vacuum or forceps delivery: Mechanical forces on the head and neck
  • C-section birth: Sudden change in pressure and positioning
  • Posterior presentation: Baby facing "sunny side up" requires more rotation
  • Cord around neck: May cause restricted neck movement
  • Multiple births: Cramped positioning in utero

Even normal, uncomplicated births involve tremendous forces on the baby's neck. Research suggests that up to 80 pounds of force can be applied to the cervical spine during delivery. This can create subtle misalignments that affect nerve function and muscle tension.

Signs of birth-related tension: Head tilted to one side, difficulty turning head both directions, arching backward, preferring to look one direction, discomfort when positioned certain ways.

How Gentle Adjustments Help

Restoring Proper Alignment

Using Talsky Tonal Chiropractic (TTC), we apply extremely gentle pressure—no more than you would use to test a ripe tomato—to address spinal misalignments. This helps:

  • Release tension in the upper cervical spine
  • Restore proper nerve communication to muscles of the mouth, jaw, and tongue
  • Improve range of motion in the neck
  • Reduce muscle tension affecting sucking mechanics

Supporting Nervous System Function

When the nervous system functions optimally, the complex coordination required for nursing becomes easier:

  • Better coordination of suck-swallow-breathe pattern
  • Improved jaw opening and tongue movement
  • Reduced fussiness and tension during feeding
  • More efficient milk transfer

The Tongue Tie Connection

An Important Consideration

Tongue ties (ankyloglossia) and lip ties are increasingly recognized as causes of latch difficulties. These occur when the tissue connecting the tongue or lip to the mouth is too tight or short, restricting movement.

How chiropractic fits in:

  • Before revision: Releasing tension can help prepare the body and optimize nerve function
  • After revision: Care helps the body adapt to new range of motion and prevents compensatory patterns
  • Without revision: If parents choose not to revise, we can help maximize function within structural limitations

If tongue tie is suspected, we can refer you to qualified providers for evaluation. We also provide care before and after tongue tie releases to optimize outcomes.
