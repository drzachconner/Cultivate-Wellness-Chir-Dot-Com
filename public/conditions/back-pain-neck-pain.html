Spinal Health & the Nervous System

Why Your Spine Matters

Your spine does more than just hold you upright. It protects your spinal cord, the primary pathway of your nervous system. When vertebrae become misaligned (what chiropractors call subluxations), they can:

  • Irritate spinal nerves: Leading to pain, numbness, or tingling
  • Create muscle tension: As your body compensates for the imbalance
  • Reduce mobility: Making movement painful or restricted
  • Affect organ function: Since nerves control all body systems
  • Trigger inflammation: Contributing to chronic pain cycles

By restoring proper spinal alignment and nervous system function, we address not just the symptoms but the underlying cause of your pain.

Types of Pain We Address

Acute Pain

Recent onset pain from injury, strain, or sudden movement. Often responds quickly to care when addressed promptly.

Chronic Pain

Long-standing pain lasting months or years. Our approach helps break the cycle of chronic discomfort and dysfunction.

Disc Issues

Bulging, herniated, or degenerative disc conditions. Gentle care can help reduce pressure and support healing.

Muscle Tension

Tight, knotted muscles in the back and neck. Proper spinal alignment allows muscles to relax naturally.

Radiating Pain

Pain that travels into arms, shoulders, hips, or legs. Often caused by nerve irritation that chiropractic can address.

Tech Neck

Forward head posture from device use. We help restore proper cervical curve and alignment.

Prevention Tips

Daily Habits for a Healthy Spine

  • Mind your posture: Keep ears over shoulders, shoulders over hips
  • Take movement breaks: Get up and move every 30-60 minutes
  • Set up your workspace: Screen at eye level, feet flat on floor
  • Sleep smart: Use supportive pillow, avoid stomach sleeping
  • Lift properly: Bend at knees, keep load close to body
  • Stay active: Regular exercise strengthens supporting muscles
  • Manage stress: Tension often settles in the neck and shoulders
  • Stay hydrated: Your spinal discs need water to stay healthy
  • Get regular adjustments: Maintenance care prevents problems
